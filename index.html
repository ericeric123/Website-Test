Key areas of cyber security this quiz covers

There are a five controls that cover the basics of effective information security, and they can be implemented by anyone regardless of their information security knowledge.

Firewalls

A firwewall is responsible for monitoring incoming and outgoing traffic from your network. It can permit or block data packets based on the security rules configured within it's settings. The main purpose of the firewall is to create a barrier protecting you from malicious hackers and viruses by securing internal network and incoming traffic.

User access Control

Access control is vital component of securing your data. By adopting appropriate controls you can monitor and decide who is allowed to use certain information and resources. Correct authentication and authorization policies can ensure that users are the person who they claim to be and that they are allowed to access the data.

Malware protection

Malware protection provides a very important layer of protection for your devices. Good antivirus software is one of the main components of the defenses that each device should have installed. Monitoring web and warning about potentialy malicious sites or scanning your device for any unwanted or harmful programs are two great advantages.

Secure Configuration

Secure configuration is a good baseline for cyber security. It refers to the security measures implemented when configuring the systesm. Misconfigurations in the security are one of the most common vulnerabilities which malicious actors can exploit to get access to the data.

Patch management

Patch management is the activity of applying software updates to your system or software. These patches are important to fix errors or improve security of the software. It is important to deploy any security updates within 14 days of release.

Why strong passwords are important?

Passwords are at the forefront of the defense against unauthorized access to both your device and personal information. The stronger your password the better security your computer will have from hackers and malicious software. You should always have password consisting of at least 8 characters and difficult to guess.

What makes a good password?

Strong password are usually longer than eight characters. They should also contain a variety of letters, numbers and symbols. Use of password manager is also one of the recommended solutions as it will create distinct passwords for all of the services you use and store them for you.
